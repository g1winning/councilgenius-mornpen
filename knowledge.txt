=== WASTE AND RECYCLING ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Rubbish-Recycling

**Bin Types and Collection**
The Shire provides four bin types:
- Red bin: General rubbish (collected weekly)
- Yellow bin: Recycling (collected fortnightly, or weekly during summer Dec-Jan)
- Green bin: Garden waste (collected fortnightly, or weekly during summer Dec-Jan)
- Brown bin: Food and green waste FOGO (food organics, garden organics) — available where offered

**Find Your Bin Day**
Use the My Neighbourhood lookup tool to find:
- Exact collection day and week for your address
- Bin types available at your property
- Holiday and special collection schedules

Access through: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/My-Neighbourhood

**Extra Bins and Capacity Upgrades**
Cost: $95 per bin per year (2025-26)
Apply through: Online form or phone [5950 1020](tel:59501020)
Examples: Extra recycling bin during summer, extra green waste during garden season

**Missed or Damaged Bins**
Report online or phone [5950 1020](tel:59501020)
Replacement cost: Nil if damaged by collection vehicle, small fee for lost bins

**Bin Lid Colour Change**
Request colour change for recycling or green bin through online form or phone [5950 1020](tel:59501020)

**Summer Weekly Recycling**
During December and January, recycling and garden waste collections move to weekly (instead of fortnightly) to manage increased holiday waste.

**Contamination Rules**
What contaminates recycling (DO NOT PUT IN YELLOW BIN):
- Plastic bags (they jam sorting equipment)
- Broken glass and ceramics
- Food and liquid residue
- Soil and garden matter
- Polystyrene and foam
- E-waste, batteries, light bulbs
- Metal paint cans, syringes

Result: Contaminated loads can close the sorting facility or require manual sorting, adding cost.

**Bin Presentation Rules**
- Bins must be placed at kerbside by 6am on collection day
- Bins returned to storage within 48 hours
- Maximum fill level: Do not overfill or compacting lid cannot close
- Lids must be closed before collection

**Complaints**
Report dirty or damaged bins to [5950 1020](tel:59501020)

=== CONTAINER DEPOSIT SCHEME ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Rubbish-Recycling/Recycling

**10 Cent Refund Scheme**
Victorian container deposit scheme: Receive 10 cents per eligible container returned.

Eligible containers:
- Drink bottles (plastic, glass, metal)
- Cans (aluminium, steel)
- Cartons (juice, milk, wine)
- Must have 'Deposit' marked on label

**Where to Return**
Return-It refund stations across the peninsula — find locations at: https://www.returnit.com.au/

Process:
1. Collect eligible containers
2. Sort and bag (or in crate)
3. Take to Return-It station
4. Scan barcode or employee scans
5. Instant refund (cash or card)

**Tips**
- Check label for Deposit mark before collecting
- Rinse containers (hygiene and processing)
- Return regularly — containers can degrade outdoors
- Reward scheme: Some Return-It stations offer loyalty programs

=== RATES AND PAYMENTS ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Rates-Valuations/Rate-payments

**How Rates Are Calculated**
Rates are calculated using the Capital Improved Value (CIV) method:
- CIV = market value of land + improvements
- Rate in the dollar (2025-26): 0.001414 cents
- Waste Service Charge: $433.00 (applies equally to all properties)

Example: Property with CIV of $925,000
= ($925,000 x 0.001414) + $433 = $1,740.95

**Rate Due Dates (2025-26)**

If paying in full:
- Due by 15 February 2026

If paying by 4 instalments:
- 1st instalment: 30 September 2025
- 2nd instalment: 30 November 2025
- 3rd instalment: 28 February 2026
- 4th instalment: 31 May 2026

If first instalment not paid by 30 Sept 2025, property reverts to full payment due 15 Feb 2026.

=== ANIMALS AND PETS ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Animals-and-Pets/Responsible-pet-ownership

**Registration (Mandatory)**
All cats and dogs aged 12 weeks or older must be registered with the Shire.

Renewal deadline: 10 April each year

Registration funds:
- Animal management staffing and after-hours services
- Investigation of unregistered animals, wandering cats/dogs, barking dogs
- Investigation and prosecution of dog attacks
- Declaration of dangerous, menacing, and restricted breed dogs
- Lifetime registration tags for registered dogs and cats
- Compulsory State Government levy per registered pet

**Microchipping (Mandatory)**
- Required before registration for all dogs and cats
- Mandatory since May 2007
- 15-digit microchip number
- Benefits: Cannot be lost, enables recovery from anywhere in Australia 24/7
- Proof of microchipping from vet required at registration

**Desexing**
All cats must be desexed and microchipped prior to registration.

Dogs and cats must be desexed before being sold or given away (except if animal is 10 years or older).

Proof of desexing from vet required at registration to receive discounted registration fee.

Benefits:
- Male dogs less likely to fight, wander, or exhibit offensive sexual habits
- Female dogs protected from breast cancer and uterus problems
- Prevents unwanted pregnancies
- Cats live longer, healthier lives
- Cats less likely to suffer anti-social behaviour, wander, or run away
- Confined cats have lower risk of FIV infection

**Confinement Rules**

Cats: 24-hour curfew
- Cats must be confined to owner's property at all times of day and night
- If found at large or trespassing, Shire Officers may take enforcement action

Dogs: On-leash in public except in designated leash-free areas
- Unless in designated leash-free area, dogs must be on leash in all public places
- Dog prohibited areas exist throughout the Shire
- If found at large or not securely confined, Shire Officers may take enforcement action
- Interactive map available for leash-free and prohibited areas

=== DOGS ON THE PENINSULA ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Animals-and-Pets/Dogs-on-the-Peninsula

**Leash Rule**
Dogs must always be under effective control of a chain, cord, or leash whilst in a public place except in designated leash-free areas.

**Dog Prohibited Areas**
Dogs are NOT permitted in dog prohibited areas whether on leash or not.

The Peninsula is home to vulnerable, threatened, and endangered species:
- Threatened Hooded Plover
- Migratory shorebirds
- Marsupials: White-footed Dunnart, Long-nosed Bandicoot
- Reptiles: Blue-tongued Lizard

Many species are susceptible to dog attacks and distressed by dog scent and barking.

**Leash-Free Areas**
Designated leash-free areas exist throughout the Mornington Peninsula:
- Dog owners must leash dogs until reaching the designated area
- Must re-leash when leaving the area
- Check interactive map for exact locations

Suggest a new leash-free area: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Online-Forms-and-Services/Leash-Free-Site-Nomination

**Responsible Dog Ownership**
When heading out with your dog:
- Follow local signage instructions
- Collect and dispose of droppings responsibly (failure may result in fines)
- Ensure your dog doesn't disturb other people, animals, and wildlife
- Regular exercise and positive stimulation reduce barking and nuisance behaviour

**Dogs at Beaches**

Port Phillip Bay and Western Port beaches
- Managed by Department of Energy, Environment and Climate Action (DEECA)
- Various dog controls apply — check interactive map for specific beach rules

Bass Strait beaches
- Managed by Parks Victoria (Mornington Peninsula National Park and Point Nepean National Park)
- Dogs prohibited at all times of year
- Council does not manage these parks

**Dogs Policy Update**
- Updated: 3 December 2024
- New controls in effect: 12 December 2024
- Full details: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Online-Forms-and-Services/Dogs-Policy-Updates

**Dog Prohibited Area Review**
Some dog-prohibited reserves show "Under Review" signs — Council is reviewing dog controls with independent consultant.
Community invited for input via online survey and outreach sessions (details coming soon).
Updated: March 2026

=== PLANNING AND PERMITS ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Building-Planning/Planning

**Do I Need a Planning Permit?**
Many activities DO NOT require a planning permit:
- Home extensions (in some cases)
- Swimming pools (up to certain size)
- Decks and patios (in some cases)
- Some garden structures

Use the planning scheme check tool to verify: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Building-Planning/Planning/Do-I-need-a-planning-permit

For specific advice, contact Pre-Application Service (see below).

**Planning Permit Application Process**

Step 1: Check if permit needed
- Use online tool or contact Pre-Application Service

Step 2: Prepare application
- Use standard forms
- Collect required drawings, plans, and specifications
- Pay application fee (based on proposal cost)

Step 3: Submit application
- Online portal or in person at Service Centres
- Reference number issued

Step 4: Assessment
- Council assesses proposal against planning scheme
- Officers may request more information
- Neighbours may be consulted (notification period)

Step 5: Decision
- Approval granted (with or without conditions)
- Refusal issued with reasons
- Deemed decision (if no decision made within statutory period)

Step 6: Objection (if refused)
- Residents can object within set period
- Objection can be lodged with Planning Panels Victoria

Typical timeframe: 30-60 days (complex applications may take longer)

**Pre-Application Service**
Get confidential advice before submitting formal application:
- Discuss your proposal with planning officer
- Understand requirements and design expectations
- Identify any issues early
- May prevent costly rejections or delays

Cost: Usually free or small fee (enquire with council)
Book: Phone [5950 1000](tel:59501000) or through online booking system

**Planning Application Fees**
Fees based on estimated project cost:
- Projects under $50,000: Lower fee
- Projects $50,000-$500,000: Mid-range fee
- Projects over $500,000: Higher fee

Current fee schedule: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Building-Planning/Planning/Planning-applications-forms-and-fees

**Planning Scheme and Zoning**
Check what's permitted on your land:
1. Identify zone (residential, commercial, agricultural, etc.)
2. Check overlays (heritage, flood, bushfire, etc.)
3. Confirm requirements (setbacks, height, parking)

Interactive planning scheme map: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Building-Planning/Planning/Check-your-planning-scheme

**Objecting to a Planning Decision**
If a permit is refused or you disagree with conditions:
- Lodge objection with Planning Panels Victoria
- Objection period: 28 days from decision letter
- Enquire about mediation option first

Contact: Planning Panels Victoria 1300 744 346

**Contact Information**
Phone: [5950 1000](tel:59501000) (press 1 for Planning)
Email: [planning@mornpen.vic.gov.au](mailto:planning@mornpen.vic.gov.au)
Online: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Building-Planning/Planning

=== BUILDING ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Building-Planning/Building

**Building Permits (Mandatory)**
A building permit is required for most construction work including:
- New houses
- Home extensions
- Decks, pergolas, sheds (larger structures)
- Swimming pools and spas
- Renovations and alterations

Some smaller items may not require a permit — check online or contact Building Services.

**Building Permit Application Process**

Step 1: Design preparation
- Engage builder or architect
- Prepare engineering drawings compliant with Building Code

Step 2: Apply for permit
- Submit drawings and specifications
- Pay application fee
- Include engineer certification if required

Step 3: Assessment
- Council reviews against Victorian Building Code
- Officers may request corrections
- Permit issued once compliant

Step 4: Construction
- Work must comply with permit conditions
- Inspections required at key stages

Step 5: Final certification
- Occupancy permit issued upon completion
- Certificate of Occupancy allows property use

=== SHORT-TERM RENTAL ACCOMMODATION ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Short-stay-rental-accommodation

**Definition and Requirements**
Short-term rental accommodation (STRA) includes:
- Entire dwellings rented for periods under 90 days continuous occupancy
- Holiday lets, Airbnb-style properties, holiday homes
- Caravan parks and holiday parks (separate regulations)
- Bed and breakfasts

Planning approval is required for STRA in most zones.

**Planning Approval Process**
1. Check if property is eligible — some zones prohibit STRA
2. Prepare application with floor plans and management details
3. Submit online or at council offices — $1,000-$2,000 application fee typical
4. Council notification to neighbours (30-day objection period)
5. Council planning assessment (4-6 weeks typical)
6. Decision — approved, refused, or conditions imposed

Contact: [5950 1000](tel:59501000)

**Management Plan Requirements**
Approved STRA must have:
- Waste management strategy (bins, recycling, garden waste)
- Parking management plan
- Noise attenuation measures
- Maximum number of guests and vehicles
- Check-in/check-out procedures
- Noise complaint contact and response protocol
- Site-specific management conditions

**Complaints About STRA**
Complaints can be made about:
- Noise (during hours of occupation)
- Parking breaches (overflow parking, guest vehicles)
- Waste mismanagement
- Breach of planning conditions
- Neighbour nuisance

Report online: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Contact-Us/Report-a-Problem
Tick "Short stay rental complaints"
Phone: [1300 850 600](tel:1300850600)

Complaints investigated — council can issue directions to owner/manager.

**Key Local Law References**
- Planning and Mandatory Parking Requirements Local Law 2018
- Community Amenity Local Law 2022 (noise, waste, neighbour disturbance)

=== COASTAL PROTECTION AND EROSION ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Coastal-protection

**Coastal Hazard Risk**
Mornington Peninsula coastline is subject to:
- Erosion (gradual land loss to sea)
- Storm surge (rapid inundation during severe weather)
- Sea level rise (long-term climate change)
- Rock seawalls and revetments (aging infrastructure)

Properties at risk:
- Within 200m of high tide mark on eroding coasts
- Below 5m elevation on exposed coasts
- Near outlets and estuaries (tidal influence)

Check your risk: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Coastal-protection

**Coastal Vulnerability Assessment**
Council provides free vulnerability assessments for:
- Erosion risk analysis
- Structural safety of existing protection works
- Suitable adaptation options
- Insurance and finance implications

Booking: [5950 1020](tel:59501020)

**Existing Protection Works**
Council maintains public seawalls and revetments. Private owners may have:
- Seawalls or revetments (council approval needed for maintenance/replacement)
- Groynes or offshore structures (varies by location)
- Vegetation stabilization (native plants, geotextiles)

Maintenance: Property owner responsibility. Get approval before major works.

**Property Adaptation Options**
- Elevation of buildings (pier-and-post, fill raise)
- Relocation (moving building inland)
- Accommodation (accepting hazard, improved insurance)
- Coastline restoration (dune rehabilitation, native planting)

Cost varies significantly. Grants may be available.

Contact: Coastal Manager [5950 1020](tel:59501020)

**Insurance Implications**
Coastal properties may face:
- Higher premiums or excesses
- Exclusion clauses for specific hazards
- Annual increases due to risk
- Difficulty obtaining insurance in high-risk areas

Check property insurer's coastal policy terms. Council cannot advise on insurance.

=== PROPERTY VALUATION AND OBJECTIONS ===
SOURCE_URL: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Rates-Valuations

**How Property is Valued**
Capital Improved Value (CIV) method:
- Market value of land at valuation date
- Plus value of improvements (buildings, structures, features)
- Assessed by independent valuer (Valuer-General Victoria)
- Updated triennially (every 3 years) — next revaluation 2024-25

Your property valuation notice includes:
- Land value
- Improvements value
- Total CIV
- Right to object procedure and deadline

**Objecting to Valuation**
Right to object if believe valuation is incorrect:
- Must lodge within 60 days of receiving notice
- Written objection explaining why valuation is wrong
- Include supporting evidence (comparable sales, property condition)
- Can provide photos, expert reports

Submit: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Rates-Valuations/Rates-Valuations-Forms
Or post to: Rates and Valuations, Mornington Peninsula Shire

Contact: [5950 1000](tel:59501000)

**Valuation Appeal**
If council upholds valuation:
- Can appeal to Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal (VCAT)
- 30-day appeal period
- VCAT makes binding decision

Council will support process and provide documentation.

**Demolition Permits**
Demolition of buildings requires:
- Demolition permit (building consent not required if full demolition)
- Asbestos survey and removal by licensed contractor (pre-demolition)
- Site remediation after demolition
- Disposal of waste at licensed facilities

Apply: [5950 1035](tel:59501035)
Contact: Building Services [building@mornpen.vic.gov.au](mailto:building@mornpen.vic.gov.au)

**Abandoned Buildings**
Buildings in poor condition or unsafe:
- Council may issue compliance order (repair or demolish)
- Owner has set period to comply
- Non-compliance can lead to prosecution
- Council can carry out work and recover costs

Report: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Contact-Us/Report-a-Problem

=== COMMON COUNCIL SERVICE SCENARIOS ===

**Scenario 1: I Want to Build an Extension**
Q: What do I need to do?
A: You'll need planning approval and building permit. First, check if your area allows extensions (some heritage overlays have restrictions). Contact council: [5950 1000](tel:59501000) for a pre-application meeting. Submit plans with engineering details. Processing time is usually 8-12 weeks. Costs vary by project size — typical application fee $1,000-$2,500. Once approved, you'll need a building permit before starting works.

**Scenario 2: My Bin Wasn't Collected**
Q: What if my bin was missed on collection day?
A: Log into My Neighbourhood or check your bin collection calendar at https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Rubbish-Recycling. Make sure bin is at kerbside by 6am. If genuinely missed, report it: phone [5950 1020](tel:59501020) or use online form. They'll arrange collection within 2-3 days. If bin is damaged by collection vehicle, it's replaced free. Make sure bins don't have lids jammed and are not overfull.

**Scenario 3: I Have a Barking Dog Problem**
Q: My neighbour's dog barks constantly. What can I do?
A: First, try talking to your neighbour calmly. If that doesn't work, contact council: [5950 1434](tel:59501434) or [animal.changes@mornpen.vic.gov.au](mailto:animal.changes@mornpen.vic.gov.au). You can lodge a complaint — council will investigate and may issue a compliance direction to the owner. Document barking times/dates. Barking is an offense under Community Amenity Local Law 2022. Council aims to resolve within 30 days.

**Scenario 4: Planning Permission for Business at Home**
Q: I want to run a small online business from home — do I need approval?
A: Most home-based businesses are exempt from planning approval if they meet criteria: no signage, no separate client visits, maximum 2 employees, no heavy vehicles, work during normal hours (7am-10pm), limited noise. Contact council: [5950 1000](tel:59501000) to confirm your business is exempt. If not exempt, you'll need planning approval (8-12 weeks, ~$1,000 fee). Email details: [planning@mornpen.vic.gov.au](mailto:planning@mornpen.vic.gov.au).

**Scenario 5: I Disagree With My Rates**
Q: I think my rates are too high — what's the appeal process?
A: Rates are based on your property's Capital Improved Value (CIV). If you believe valuation is incorrect, object within 60 days of receiving notice at: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Your-Property/Rates-Valuations. Include supporting evidence (comparable sales, property condition). Council will investigate. If you disagree with council's decision, you can appeal to VCAT within 30 days. Contact: [5950 1000](tel:59501000) or [rates@mornpen.vic.gov.au](mailto:rates@mornpen.vic.gov.au).

**Scenario 6: Beach Unsafe (Poor Water Quality)**
Q: Is it safe to swim at the beach today?
A: Check Safe Beaches Victoria: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Community/Beaches for latest status. Council monitors water quality at popular swimming beaches. Avoid swimming if:
- Bacteria (E. coli) counts are high
- Blue-green algal bloom posted
- Sewage overflow warning
- After heavy rain (48 hours for stormwater to clear)
Beach status is updated regularly — check before you go or call [1300 850 600](tel:1300850600).

**Scenario 7: Building Hazard — Tree Falling**
Q: A large tree on council land is hanging over my house — it's dangerous.
A: Report immediately: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Contact-Us/Report-a-Problem (select "Tree issues") or phone [5950 1020](tel:59501020). Describe danger (limbs over house, disease signs, dead branches). If emergency: 000 if imminent risk to life. Council will assess and schedule removal or pruning. If your tree is leaning onto council land, council may contact you about removal costs (varies by circumstances).

**Scenario 8: Neighbourhood Complaint — Noise**
Q: My neighbours have loud parties late at night. Can council help?
A: First, document noise (time, date, duration, what type). Try friendly discussion. If continues, lodge complaint: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Contact-Us/Report-a-Problem (select "Noise complaints") or call [1300 850 600](tel:1300850600). Provide specific details. Council will investigate — officers may attend and issue direction. Repeat breaches can result in fines up to $600 under Community Amenity Local Law 2022. For emergencies (violence risk): 000.

**Scenario 9: Pothole in Road**
Q: There's a dangerous pothole on my street. How long until it's fixed?
A: Report immediately: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/Contact-Us/Report-a-Problem (select "Potholes and road issues") or phone [5950 1020](tel:59501020). Include location and size. Council prioritizes by danger level: hazard (immediate = 24 hrs), urgent (1-3 weeks), routine (4-8 weeks). Major roads repaired faster than minor streets. Call [1300 850 600](tel:1300850600) if patch fails — council will return.

**Scenario 10: Wildlife in Roof**
Q: I have possums/bats in my roof — how do I get them out?
A: DO NOT trap animals yourself — they're protected. Contact licensed wildlife handler or local vet for relocation. Cost typically $300-$800. Once animal removed, seal entry points (8mm mesh) to prevent return. Council can refer you to licensed removalists: [5950 1020](tel:59501020). Prevention: trim overhanging branches, secure gutters, install one-way doors during exclusion season (January-April).

**Scenario 11: Planning a Large Party/Event at Home**
Q: I want to host a large garden party. Are there rules I need to follow?
A: For gatherings on private property, follow Community Amenity Local Law 2022:
- Noise must not be unreasonable or louder than normal conversation after 9pm
- Event must not cause nuisance to neighbours
- Adequate parking on-site
- End by midnight (or earlier in quiet hours)
- Notify neighbours as courtesy
If neighbours complain, council may issue compliance direction. For events on council land, hire permits required: https://mornpen.bookable.net.au or [5950 1020](tel:59501020).

**Scenario 12: Moving House — New Rates and Services**
Q: I'm moving to Mornington Peninsula — what council services do I need to know?
A: Key first steps:
1. Find your address details: https://www.mornpen.vic.gov.au/My-Neighbourhood
2. Register for bin services: [5950 1020](tel:59501020) — moving costs ~$150
3. Check rates — first bill arrives with notice from council
4. Update address: [customerservice@mornpen.vic.gov.au](mailto:customerservice@mornpen.vic.gov.au)
5. If pets: register dogs/cats within 10 days (mandatory)
6. Check planning restrictions for property use
Contact: [1300 850 600](tel:1300850600) for orientation call.
